# Meridian: The Default Prevention Agent
## Don't just calculate cost. Prevent financial ruin.

---

## The Thesis

Every existing real estate tool asks: "Can you afford the monthly payment?"

That's the wrong question. The right question is: **"Can you survive owning this property for 10 years, through rate renewal shocks, hidden assessments, and unexpected income disruption, without defaulting?"**

Canada is sitting on a slow-moving crisis. Mortgage arrears in Toronto climbed 45% year-over-year in 2025. The national 90-day delinquency rate hit 0.33% in Q1 2026, the highest since 2019, and it's still climbing. Roughly 1.15 million Canadian mortgages are renewing in 2026 at rates well above what borrowers originally signed. Non-mortgage delinquencies (car loans, credit cards) have reached levels not seen since 2009.

Behind every arrears statistic is a family that bought a home they thought they could afford based on the list price. They didn't know about the special assessment coming for their aging condo. They didn't factor in the $12K/year transport cost of a car-dependent suburb. They didn't stress-test against a rate shock at renewal.

Meridian prevents that.

## Two Layers of the Problem

### Layer 1: Hidden Property Costs (City Data)
The foundation. A list price hides:
- Land transfer tax ($20K-$50K one-time, invisible until closing)
- Property tax trajectory (rising with neighbourhood intensification)
- Flood zone insurance loading (+$3,500/yr)
- Heritage designation restrictions (renovation delays and costs)
- Condo special assessment risk (building age + permit history proxy)
- Development pressure signals (future tax increases)
- Transport cost differential (car-dependent suburb vs. transit-aligned)

This layer uses Toronto Open Data. Deterministic, auditable, no opinions.

### Layer 2: Personal Financial Resilience (Flinks)
A property might be affordable on paper. But can THIS buyer afford THIS property given their actual financial situation?

Flinks connects to the buyer's bank accounts (with consent) and provides:

**What Flinks gives us (4,500+ attributes):**
- Verified income (employment, government, other sources)
- Monthly cash flow patterns and volatility
- Existing debt obligations (loan payments, credit cards)
- NSF frequency (insufficient funds, a stress signal)
- Savings rate and emergency fund buffer
- Income stability over time (trending up, flat, or declining)

**What we compute from it:**
- True debt-to-income ratio (not self-reported, bank-verified)
- Monthly surplus after all obligations (how thin is the margin?)
- Emergency runway: months of mortgage payments covered by liquid savings
- Cash flow volatility score: how predictable is their income?

**Why emergency runway is the key metric:**
It doesn't matter whether the shock is a job loss, an illness, a recession, or an industry disruption. If a buyer has 12 months of emergency runway, they survive any of these. If they have 2 months, they're fragile regardless of cause. Emergency runway is a universal resilience measure that avoids speculating about what specific shock might hit.

## Product Versions: V1, V2, V3

**Ship by:** Sunday May 31, 4pm

The core promise: Enter a Toronto address and list price, get the true 10-year cost with hidden flags.

| Feature | Data Source | Complexity | Status |
|---------|-----------|------------|--------|
| LTT calculator (Ontario + Toronto MLTT) | Hardcoded brackets | Low | Day 1 |
| First-time buyer rebate ($8,475) | Hardcoded | Low | Day 1 |
| Property tax estimate (10-year projection) | Hardcoded rate 0.767% | Low | Day 1 |
| Heritage designation lookup (Part IV/V/Listed) | Toronto Open Data CKAN | Medium | Day 1 |
| RentSafeTO building score | Toronto Open Data CKAN | Medium | Day 1 |
| TRCA flood zone check | ArcGIS API | Medium | Day 1 |
| Development application density (500m) | Toronto Open Data CKAN | Medium | Day 1 |
| Building permits red/yellow flags | Toronto Open Data CKAN | Medium | Day 1 |
| Mortgage cost: 3 rate scenarios (base/bear/bull) | Deterministic math | Low | Day 1 |

**V1 architecture:**

- Agent 2: Property Risk Engine (parallel CKAN + ArcGIS queries)
- Agent 3: Cost Computation (deterministic math)
- Agent 4: LLM Synthesis (Llama 3.1 8B on GB10 via NIM)

**Target launch:** July 2026

| Feature | Data Source | What It Adds |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Flinks Connect integration | Flinks API (OAuth) | Bank-verified income, debt, cash flow |
| Emergency runway calculation | Flinks: savings / monthly housing cost | Universal resilience metric |
| True debt-to-income ratio | Flinks: verified income + obligations | Bank-verified, not self-reported |
| Cash flow volatility score | Flinks: 12-month pattern analysis | How predictable is the buyer's income? |
| Rate renewal stress test | Flinks + mortgage scenarios | "Under the bear scenario, can your cash flow absorb a $900/month jump?" |
| Resilience verdict (green/yellow/red) | All layers combined | Single clear answer |

**V2 verdict framework:**

| Verdict | Emergency Runway | Debt-to-Income (Bear) | Hidden Cost Flags | Message |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------|---------|
| GREEN | 6+ months | Below 35% | Absorbed in surplus | "You can weather the storms. Sound 10-year hold." |
| YELLOW | 3-6 months | 35-42% | 1-2 flags present | "Affordable today, limited margin for shocks. Consider building a larger buffer." |
| RED | Under 3 months | Above 42% | Multiple flags | "Defaulting within 10 years is a material risk. Renting may be stronger right now." |

**V2 privacy model:**
- Data processed locally on device (nothing stored after session)
- User explicitly consents via Flinks Connect widget
- No credentials touch Meridian's system
- Aligns with Canada's open banking framework (launching 2026)

**V2 architecture update:**
- Agent 3 becomes: Personal Affordability Engine (Flinks data + stress test)

## Pitch Script (Final)

> Judges, 1.15 million Canadians are renewing their mortgages this year at higher rates. Toronto arrears are up 45%. Behind every default is a family that didn't know what they were getting into.
>
> A list price doesn't tell you what a property really costs. It hides land transfer taxes, heritage restrictions, flood risk, rising neighbourhood assessments, and the true cost of car dependency. These hidden costs push families into default years after they buy.
>
> Meridian is an AI agent that takes any Toronto address and reveals the true 10-year cost using 6 City of Toronto Open Data sources. Today, it flags the hidden risks. In Version 2, it connects to your bank via Flinks to answer the harder question: can YOU survive owning this property through a rate shock, with your actual savings and cash flow? And in Version 3, it tells you whether you should buy or rent, and where in Toronto you get the most value for your budget.
>
> Everything runs locally on the ASUS GX10. No financial data leaves the device. The data is public and auditable. And the product has the courage to tell you the truth: sometimes, the smartest financial move is not to buy.

## The Default Cascade (for the pitch deck)

1. Family buys a home based on list price and current income
2. Hidden costs erode their margin (special assessments, tax increases, car dependency)
3. External shock hits (rate renewal at +1.5%, income disruption, recession)
4. Family defaults on mortgage
5. Credit destroyed for 7 years. Forced to rent at higher cost. Children's stability disrupted.
6. Lender absorbs loss, tightens future lending standards
7. Fewer buyers in market, prices drop, neighbouring homeowners lose equity
8. Municipal tax revenue declines as assessed values fall
9. City cuts services, neighbourhood quality drops

**One family's default is not isolated.** It's a cascade that damages the community and the city's economic base.

**Meridian breaks the cascade at step 1** by giving the family the truth before they sign.

### Criterion 2: Agentic Design
**Event tagline:** "Your Agents. Your Models. Your Edge. Build autonomous systems that think, act, and run anywhere."

| Requirement | Meridian Alignment | Strength |
|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| Agentic (multi-step autonomous reasoning) | 4-agent pipeline: intake, parallel data retrieval across 6+ APIs, cost computation, LLM synthesis. Agents decide which flags to raise based on data. | STRONG |
| Orchestration | Agent 2 does async fan-out to 6 data sources in parallel, merges results. Agent 4 synthesizes structured data into natural language. | STRONG |
| Autonomous decision-making | The verdict (green/yellow/red in V2) is an autonomous recommendation, not just data display. The agent has an opinion. | MODERATE (V1 shows data), STRONG (V2 gives verdict) |

**GAP:** V1 is more of a data pipeline than a truly agentic system. The agents are deterministic (no LLM reasoning in agents 1-3). Judges who expect agents that reason, plan, and adapt may see this as a sophisticated API aggregator rather than an agentic system.

**Mitigation:**
1. Make Agent 4 (LLM) do real reasoning, not just template filling. Give it the structured data and let it decide what to emphasize, what to warn about, and what to recommend. Show the judge that the LLM output varies meaningfully based on the property's risk profile.
2. Add a conversational follow-up layer: after the report, the user can ask questions ("What if I put 25% down instead of 20%?") and the agent re-computes and re-synthesizes. This turns a one-shot report into an agentic conversation.
3. Consider adding a planning step where Agent 1 decides which data sources are relevant (e.g., skip RentSafeTO for freehold houses, skip heritage for new builds). This shows adaptive behavior.
